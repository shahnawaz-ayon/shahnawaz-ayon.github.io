How to make Dhaka a sustainable city

Dhaka, the capital city of Bangladesh, is one of the most densely populated cities in the world, with a population of over 21 million people. The rapid growth of the city has led to numerous challenges, including traffic congestion, air pollution, inadequate waste management, and a lack of green spaces. However, the citizens of Dhaka have the potential to transform their city into a sustainable metropolis by adopting a variety of measures. One of the most significant steps that citizens of Dhaka can take towards sustainability is to reduce their reliance on private cars and promote the use of public transportation, bicycles, and walking. The city's public transportation system needs improvement, but initiatives like the introduction of electric buses and the expansion of the metro rail network can be game-changers. Additionally, encouraging carpooling and ride-sharing can help reduce traffic congestion and air pollution. Another critical area for the citizens of Dhaka to focus on is waste management.
The city generates a significant amount of waste, but the existing infrastructure is insufficient to manage it effectively. Citizens can take steps to reduce waste by practicing recycling and composting and by reducing the use of single-use plastics. The government can also incentivize waste reduction by providing tax benefits for companies that implement sustainable waste management practices.
Dhaka's citizens can also work towards creating green spaces in the city. Parks and open spaces provide a respite from the concrete jungle and help improve air quality. Urban farming initiatives, such as community gardens and rooftop gardens, can also help promote a sustainable food system and provide fresh produce.
Water management is another area where citizens can play a crucial role. Dhaka is prone to flooding, and the city's water supply is often contaminated. Citizens can take steps to conserve water by adopting water-efficient practices, such as installing low-flow showerheads and toilets. Rainwater harvesting and wastewater treatment can also help alleviate the water crisis in the city.
Finally, citizens of Dhaka can promote sustainable development by supporting businesses that prioritize sustainability. By choosing to purchase products from companies that prioritize sustainability, citizens can help create demand for environmentally friendly products and services. Additionally, citizens can support sustainable tourism by promoting eco-tourism and responsible travel practices.
In conclusion, the citizens of Dhaka have the potential to turn their city into a sustainable metropolis. By promoting sustainable transportation, waste management, green spaces, water management, and sustainable development, citizens can contribute to a cleaner, greener, and healthier future for themselves and future generations. It will require a collective effort from the government, businesses, and citizens to turn this vision into reality, but the benefits are worth the effort.

The Importance of Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity's importance is on the rise. Fundamentally, our society is more technologically reliant than ever before and there is no sign that this trend will slow. Data leaks that could result in identity theft are now publicly posted on social media accounts. Sensitive information like social security numbers, credit card information and bank account details are now stored in cloud storage services like Dropbox or Google Drive. The fact of the matter is whether you are an individual, small business or large multinational, you rely on computer systems every day. Pair this with the rise in cloud services, poor cloud service security, smartphones and the Internet of Things (IoT) and we have a myriad of cybersecurity threats that didn't exist a few decades ago. We need to understand the difference between cybersecurity and information security, even though the skillsets are becoming more similar.
